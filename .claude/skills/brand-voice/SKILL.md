# Brand Voice Skill

## Voice Attributes

- **Direct**: Say what you mean. No jargon unless necessary. Clear cause-effect.
- **Warm**: Human, not robotic. Recognize effort. Celebrate wins.
- **Knowledgeable**: Trade-specific. Speak with authority, earned not assumed.
- **Honest**: Acknowledge limitations. No false guarantees.
- **Confident**: Coaching presence. Calm in urgent moments.
- **Engaging**: Conversation, not lecture. Questions, not statements.

## Word Choice Table

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| spot, observe, catch | identify, notice, report |
| hazard, risk | danger, threat, accident |
| coaching, guidance | training, education |
| you're doing great | excellent work, well done |
| let's fix that | you made a mistake |
| ground path | electrical pathway |
| pinch point | amputation hazard |
| crew, team | workforce, employees |
| shift start | beginning of shift |
| chat | call, contact, reach out |

## Writing Rules

1. **Contractions**: Use them. "You're" not "You are." Feels conversational.
2. **Sentences**: Short. 1–2 lines max. One idea per sentence.
3. **Voice**: Active, second person. "You spotted it" not "It was spotted."
4. **Trade language**: Use their words. Ironworkers say "rigging." Use it.
5. **Second person**: "You're becoming a spotter" not "Workers who observe."
6. **Avoid**: Passive voice, corporate tone, vague pronouns

**Bad example**:
"Observations indicate that personal protective equipment utilization could be enhanced."

**Good example**:
"That glove — it'd protect your hands in the rigging. You with me?"

## Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone | Energy | Example |
|---------|------|--------|---------|
| **Coaching** | Affirming, curious | Steady | "Exactly. That's the pinch point." |
| **Hazard response** | Direct, confident | High | "Move the ladder now. → Chat for analysis." |
| **Serious risk** | Urgent, clear | Very high | "Whoa. Lockout not engaged. Stop work." |
| **Toolbox talks** | Educational, peer | Medium | "Let's talk ground paths. Here's the math." |
| **Marketing** | Inspiring, confident | Medium | "Be the spotter. Shift the culture." |
| **Social/community** | Warm, celebratory | Medium-high | "3 hazards spotted this week. You're crushing it." |

## SMS Response Pattern: Acknowledge → Coach → Empower

**Pattern**:
1. **Acknowledge**: "You're right about that"
2. **Coach**: "Here's why it matters: [one risk layer]"
3. **Empower**: "Keep spotting. Chat if you want deeper analysis."

**Example**:
```
You caught the rigging angle. Right call—load math changes
at 45°+. You're thinking like a foreman. Keep it up.
```

Breaking down:
- Acknowledge: "You caught the rigging angle. Right call."
- Coach: "load math changes at 45°+"
- Empower: "You're thinking like a foreman. Keep it up."

## Typography

- **Headlines**: DM Sans, Bold, 28–32px (large screens), 20–24px (mobile)
- **Subheads**: DM Sans, Semibold, 18–20px
- **Body**: Inter, Regular, 14–16px
- **UI labels**: Inter, Semibold, 12–14px
- **Mobile first**: Scale down headlines for mobile

## Examples: Good vs Bad Copy

**SMS Responses**

GOOD: "Exactly. Three-point contact on that ladder. You're spotting like a journeyman."

BAD: "Your observation has been noted and logged for future reference."

---

**Marketing Headlines**

GOOD: "Text us a hazard. Get coaching in 5 seconds. Shift the culture."

BAD: "Integrated occupational safety intelligence platform leveraging mobile-first AI coaching."

---

**UI Labels**

GOOD: "Spot a hazard"

BAD: "Submit observation form"

---

**Error Messages**

GOOD: "Couldn't send that. Try again in a moment."

BAD: "HTTP 502 Gateway timeout error encountered."

---

**Onboarding Screens**

GOOD: "Your trade: Electrician. We'll focus on shock, arc flash, and lockout."

BAD: "Select occupational classification for personalized risk framework."

---

**Serious Risk Alert**

GOOD: "Stop. Confined space without ventilation. Get your crew out now."

BAD: "Warning: Potential atmospheric hazard detected in enclosed work area."

---

**Toolbox Talk Opener**

GOOD: "This week we're talking about ground paths. One electrician spotted a rigging angle issue. Here's the math behind why it matters."

BAD: "The following safety brief addresses risk mitigation strategies related to electrical grounding procedures."

---

**Feedback Loop Confirmation**

GOOD: "Thanks for spotting that pinch point last week. We fixed it and covered it in toolbox talk. You're making a difference."

BAD: "Your prior observation has been processed and disseminated through official safety channels."
