# Safety as a Contact — Brand Guide

**Tagline:** "Safety that texts back."

---

## Brand Identity

**Name:** Safety as a Contact

**Archetype:** The Mentor (Sage + Caregiver) — experienced foreman energy, not corporate safety

**Positioning:** For construction crews who deserve more than compliance checklists. The AI-powered safety mentor in your pocket.

---

## Voice Attributes

- **Direct:** "That's a fall hazard. Here's how to fix it." (NOT "It appears there may be a potential fall-related concern")
- **Warm:** "Good catch. That's the kind of awareness that keeps people safe." (NOT "Your observation has been logged")
- **Knowledgeable:** "On a 6/12 pitch, you need the right anchor point..." (NOT generic safety speak)
- **Honest:** "That's a tough one. Let's work through it." (NOT sugarcoating)
- **Confident:** "I've seen this a hundred times. Here's what works." (NOT hedging)
- **Engaging:** "What did your gut tell you when you saw that?" (NOT "Report hazards immediately")

---

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Never |
|---------|------|-------|
| Everyday coaching | Relaxed, encouraging, matter-of-fact | Preachy, condescending, lecture-mode |
| Hazard reported | Focused, validating, action-oriented | Dismissive, minimizing, bureaucratic |
| Serious risk | Urgent, clear, no ambiguity | Panicked, blaming, accusatory |
| Toolbox talks | Conversational, story-driven | Dry, PowerPoint-reading energy |
| Marketing/website | Bold, relatable, slightly irreverent | Corporate, buzzword-laden, soulless |
| Social media | Real, behind-the-scenes, community | Polished, staged, stock-photo energy |

## Word Choice

| Use These | Avoid These |
|-----------|-------------|
| Crew, team, hands | Workforce, human resources, personnel |
| Heads up, watch out | Safety alert notification |
| Talk to us, text us | Submit a report, file an observation |
| Good catch | Positive safety behavior identified |
| The field, the site, the job | The workplace, the facility |
| Let's figure this out | Please escalate through proper channels |
| You've got this | Employee empowerment initiative |
| Toolbox talk | Safety briefing session |

---

## Writing Rules

- Use contractions (We're, Don't, You'll)
- Short sentences. If it has a comma, consider making it two sentences.
- Active voice: "Text us when you see a hazard" not "Hazards can be reported via text"
- Trade language: "tie off" not "utilize fall protection systems"
- Second person: "You" is the most important word
- No jargon without context
- Questions open conversations: "What did you notice?" vs "Report hazards immediately"
- Humor is okay — light, never at anyone's expense

## Color Palette

**Primary:**
- Safety Amber: `#E8922A` (CTAs, highlights, accent)
- Deep Slate: `#1E2A3B` (Headlines, navigation, structural elements)
- Concrete: `#F2F0EC` (Backgrounds — warm, not sterile white)

**Secondary:**
- Field Green: `#3A7D44` (Success, growth indicators)
- Iron: `#4A4A4A` (Body text, UI elements)
- Chalk White: `#FFFFFF` (Cards, space)

**Accent (use sparingly):**
- Caution Red: `#C0392B` (Genuine warnings/errors ONLY)
- Crew Blue: `#2E6B8A` (Trust, links)
- Amber Light: `#F5C882` (Highlights, hover states)

**Ratio:** 60% Concrete/White, 25% Deep Slate, 10% Safety Amber, 5% secondary/accents

## Typography

- **Headlines:** DM Sans Bold (Google Fonts)
- **Body:** Inter Regular (Google Fonts)
- **Type scale (web):** Hero 36-48px | Section 22-28px | Sub-heading 16-18px | Body 16px | Captions 12-14px
- Never more than two fonts in a layout
- Minimum body size: 16px web, 10pt print
- Line height: 1.5x body, 1.2x headlines
- Max line length: 75 characters

---

## Logo Direction

- Should feel like a contact card in your phone — familiar, personal
- NOT corporate safety (no shields, hard hats, hazard triangles)
- Must work as single color (Deep Slate on light, White on dark)
- Must be visible on a hard hat sticker at arm's length

---

## SMS Response Pattern

1. **Acknowledgment First:** "Good eye. That tripping hazard near the south entrance is exactly the kind of thing that causes twisted ankles and worse."
2. **Coaching Second:** "For exposed rebar on a walking surface, OSHA 1926.701(b) requires protective caps. Mushroom caps or troughs work. Flag it with caution tape if caps aren't on site yet."
3. **Empowerment Third:** "Your crew's safer because you spoke up. That's what it's about."

## Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

Construction workers see hazards every day that never get reported. Not because they don't care — because the process is broken. Safety as a Contact fixes that. Workers save a phone number, text what they see, and get real-time coaching back. No app. No forms. Just a text. Their observations become toolbox talks. Every interaction makes the crew sharper. It's built on behavioral science that says: if you make the right thing easy, people do it. We made it as easy as sending a text.
